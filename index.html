Edgar Caballero

I am a Ph.D. student in Animal Sciences at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, working at the intersection of bioinformatics, genomics, and machine learning.

My research focuses on synthetic genotype generation, population genetics, and computational methods for understanding large-scale genomic data. I am especially interested in generative models, statistical genetics, and the application of AI to animal breeding and genomics.

This website highlights my research, publications, academic work, and ongoing projects.

Research

My work combines computational genomics, deep learning, and statistical genetics to study and simulate complex biological systems.

Current projects include generative adversarial networks for synthetic SNP data, evaluation of linkage disequilibrium and allele frequency preservation, and PCA-based frameworks for genomic data generation and analysis.

Areas: Bioinformatics, Population Genetics, Animal Genomics
Methods: GANs, WGAN-GP, PCA, statistical modeling
Tools: Python, R, PyTorch, genomic analysis pipelines
